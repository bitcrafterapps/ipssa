Persistent algae after super-chlorination

Customer A — commercial pool, ~50k gal, vinyl liner. Treated with 10 ppm shock two days ago. Still seeing green walls. CYA is 80 ppm. FC reading this morning: 0.5 ppm. pH 7.8.

James Rivera

22 min ago

High CYA is killing your chlorine efficiency. At 80 ppm you need 6–8 ppm FC to have any active sanitizer. Try a 25% partial drain first, then re-shock to 15 ppm. Let it circulate 24h before brushing walls.

Chlorination · Basic Training 1

What is the ideal free chlorine range for a residential swimming pool?

Why 1.0 – 3.0 ppm?

This range provides effective sanitation without irritating swimmers. Below 1.0 ppm, pathogens may not be killed fast enough. Above 3.0 ppm can cause eye and skin irritation.
